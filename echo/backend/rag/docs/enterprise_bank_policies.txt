FINSERV BANK - ENTERPRISE POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

DISPUTE RESOLUTION POLICY
=========================

Dispute Resolution SLA (Service Level Agreement):
- Debit Card Transaction Disputes: Investigation completed within 7 working days. If confirmed fraud, full refund within 10 working days.
- Credit Card Disputes (Chargeback): Chargeback filed within 15 calendar days of dispute. Resolution within 60-90 days depending on card network.
- Fund Transfer Disputes: For NEFT/RTGS/UPI, investigation within 3-5 working days. If reversal needed, funds returned within 24 hours.
- Unauthorized Transactions: Full refund within 10 working days if fraud confirmed. Provisional credit may be issued immediately.

Dispute Filing Process:
1. Report within 30 days of transaction (required)
2. Provide transaction reference, approximate amount, date, and merchant details
3. Bank acknowledges within 24 hours with complaint reference number
4. Investigation initiated - customer may be asked for additional documentation
5. Findings communicated to customer with resolution status
6. If resolved in customer favor, refund processed immediately
7. If resolved against customer, detailed explanation provided in writing

Escalation Path:
- Level 1: Branch Customer Care (0-5 days)
- Level 2: Regional Office (5-15 days)
- Level 3: Corporate Complaints (15-30 days)
- Banking Ombudsman: If issue unresolved after 30 days or customer unsatisfied

Our Commitment:
- 100% liability for fraudulent debit card transactions within 24 hours of reporting
- No liability if customer shared PIN/OTP with unauthorized person
- 0% customer liability for verified unauthorized credit card transactions
- Investigation integrity: No bias toward bank or customer, third-party audits conducted annually

FRAUD REPORTING AND PREVENTION
===============================

Fraud Reporting Procedure (24-Hour Hotline):
1. Call 1800-FINSERV immediately (available 24/7, every day including holidays)
2. Report nature of fraud: card cloning, unauthorized transaction, phishing, account takeover
3. Provide transaction details: amount, date, time, merchant name, location
4. Bank references your phone call and creates Fraud Report (FR) number
5. Your card is instantly blocked by the security team
6. Investigation starts immediately for high-value fraud (>Rs. 10,000)

What to Do After Reporting:
- Block your card immediately (self-block available in app)
- Do not use the card for any transaction
- Collect evidence: emails, messages, screenshots of suspicious activity
- Change your password immediately from a different device
- Monitor account for next 30 days - report any further unusual activity
- Cooperate with investigation: provide requested documents, respond to queries

Bank's Fraud Investigation Team:
- Trained specialists analyze transaction patterns, device fingerprints, geolocation
- Coordinates with law enforcement if criminal activity suspected
- Contacts merchants to verify or decline transactions
- Uses machine learning to flag suspicious patterns
- May request customer statement, authentication records, device details

Fraud Prevention Measures We Employ:
- Real-time transaction monitoring: Detects anomalies instantly
- Device fingerprinting: Flagged if login from unusual device/location
- Behavioral analytics: Alerts on atypical spending patterns
- Encryption: All sensitive data encrypted end-to-end
- Rate limiting: Prevents rapid-fire transaction attempts
- Biometric verification: For high-value transactions
- Card tokenization: Prevents full card number exposure in online transactions
- 3D Secure authentication: Mandatory for international and high-risk online purchases

ACCOUNT DORMANCY POLICY
=======================

Dormancy Classification:
- Account becomes dormant after 24 consecutive months of no deposits or withdrawals
- Savings accounts: 2 years. Current accounts: 3 years. Loan accounts: 6 months after maturity.
- No partial activity counts (e.g., only interest credits don't prevent dormancy)

Dormancy Status Benefits & Restrictions:
- Dormant account: Limited access, restricted withdrawals, no debit card usage
- Dormant deposits and investments: Untouched but earning zero interest/returns
- Standing instructions: Automatically suspended
- Loan EMI: Account may be flagged for non-compliance if loan still active

Dormancy Notification:
- 1st Letter: Sent at 12 months - warning, request to activate
- 2nd Letter: Sent at 18 months - final notice before dormancy
- 3rd Letter: Sent at 24 months - account declared dormant, options provided
- Email notifications with reactivation instructions sent at each stage

Reactivation Process:
- Visit any branch with original ID proof and account number
- Fill Reactivation Form at branch (takes 5 minutes)
- Account is reactivated immediately by branch manager
- Full functionality restored: debit card activated, standing instructions resume, etc.
- If account was used for loan/investments, corresponding status updated
- No reactivation fee charged

Special Cases:
- Senior citizen accounts (60+): No dormancy for 10 years, then classified as dormant
- NRI accounts: No dormancy if at least one annual transaction
- Trust/Joint accounts: Dormancy if all co-owners have been inactive

Dormant Account Escheat:
- If account remains dormant for 10+ years, funds may be transferred to RBI under Unclaimed Deposits Act
- Customer can claim funds from RBI with proof of ownership
- Bank maintains records indefinitely

MINIMUM BALANCE REQUIREMENTS
=============================

Account Type-wise Minimum Balance:
1. Classic Savings Account:
   - Minimum balance: Rs. 1,000
   - Penalty for falling below: Rs. 50 per quarter
   - Applies: All-India except metros (Rs. 2,500 in metros: Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Hyderabad)

2. Premium Savings Account:
   - Minimum balance: Rs. 25,000
   - Penalty: Rs. 100 per quarter if balance drops below
   - Waived for: Senior citizens, students, first 6 months after opening

3. Salary Account (Special):
   - No minimum balance requirement
   - Zero fees on all standard transactions
   - Requirement: Salary >= Rs. 5,000 per month credited monthly
   - Benefits: Free debit card, unlimited ATM withdrawals, no maintenance charges

4. Student Account (Special):
   - No minimum balance requirement until graduation
   - After graduation (2 years): Automatically converts to Classic account with Rs. 1,000 minimum
   - Free e-pass for one sibling account
   - Benefits: Complimentary locker till Rs. 50,000 coverage

5. Current Account (Business/Corporate):
   - Minimum balance: Rs. 50,000 for standard, Rs. 1,00,000 for premium
   - Penalty: Rs. 200 per quarter if average balance requirement not met
   - Applies to: Sole proprietor, partnership, companies, organizations

Penalty Calculation:
- Penalty applied if balance falls below minimum on any day of the quarter
- Charged in the following month
- Multiple penalties can apply if balance stays below
- Penalty is deducted from account automatically

Waiver Policy:
- Senior citizens (60+): 100% minimum balance waiver
- Students: Waiver until graduation
- First-time account opening: 6 months waiver
- Account linked to disability: 50% waiver

INTERNATIONAL TRANSACTION CHARGES
==================================

Foreign Exchange (FX) Rate:
- Rates updated real-time, based on SWIFT/interbank rates
- Markup added by FinServ: 0.75% on buy (when merchant charges), 0.75% on sell (when customer receives)
- Current rate always shown before transaction confirmation

Debit Card International Transactions:
1. Overseas ATM Cash Withdrawal:
   - Charge: 1.5% of amount (minimum Rs. 100, maximum Rs. 1,000)
   - Plus FX markup: 0.75%
   - Total: ~2.25% of transaction amount
   - Limit: Rs. 2 lakhs per day, Rs. 5 lakhs per month

2. International Online Purchase (Foreign Merchant):
   - Charge: 1% of transaction amount
   - Plus FX markup: 0.75%
   - 3D Secure verification: Mandatory for security
   - No limit on frequency, but fraud checks apply

3. International Retail Purchase (Physical Card):
   - Charge: 1% of amount
   - Plus FX markup: 0.75%
   - Applicable only if card processed internationally

Credit Card International Charges:
1. Foreign ATM Cash Advance:
   - Charge: 2.5% of amount (minimum Rs. 200)
   - Plus FX markup: 0.75%
   - Interest: 3.5% per month from transaction date
   - Daily limit: 50% of credit limit

2. International Merchant Purchase:
   - Charge: 2% of amount
   - Plus FX markup: 0.75%
   - Rewards still applied: Full rewards on international spend
   - No charge for emergency cash advances (max Rs. 50,000, urgent authorization required)

Waiver Policy:
- Premium Cardholders: 50% waiver on international charges
- LHW (Large High-Worth) customers (AUM > Rs. 50 lakh): 100% waiver on all FX charges
- First international transaction: No charge (waived once per year)

International Money Transfer (Outbound):
- Channel: SWIFT transfers to foreign bank accounts
- Charge: Rs. 500 (standard), Rs. 300 (premium), Rs. 0 (LHW)
- Plus FX markup: 0.75%
- Processing time: 2-3 business days
- Limit: Up to customer's account balance, no individual transaction cap

Inbound International Transfers:
- Received amount converted to INR at receiving rate + 0.75% markup
- No charge to recipient
- Processing time: 1-2 business days depending on source country
- SWIFT: 1-2 days, IMPS partners (few countries): instant

INTEREST RATE POLICY
====================

Savings Account Interest:
- Interest rates: Varies by balance and account tier (see FAQ for current rates)
- Compounding: Quarterly (on 31st March, 30th June, 30th Sept, 31st Dec)
- Min balance for interest: Rs. 1,000 (if below, no interest for that quarter)
- Interest paid in: Same month as compounding, visible next business day
- Tax: TDS at 10% applicable if interest > Rs. 10,000 per annum

Fixed Deposit Interest:
- Rates: Vary by tenure (7 days to 10 years), reviewed quarterly
- Compounding: As per customer choice (monthly/quarterly/half-yearly/at maturity)
- Senior citizen premium: Additional 0.5% on all tenures
- Super Senior (80+): Additional 0.75%
- Interest payout: Via standing instruction to savings account or cash withdrawal

Recurring Deposit (RD):
- Duration: 6 months to 10 years, minimum monthly deposit Rs. 500
- Interest: Compounded quarterly, calculated on accumulated amount
- Rate: Same as FD for equivalent tenure
- Maturity: Lump sum of all deposits + accumulated interest
- Loan against RD: Available at RD rate + 2%

Loan Interest Rates:
- Personal Loan: 8.5% to 18% p.a. (floating, reviewed quarterly with repo rate)
- Home Loan: 6.5% to 9.5% p.a. based on LTV (Loan-to-Value ratio)
- Auto Loan: 7.5% to 11% p.a.
- Credit Card: 3.5% per month (42% p.a.) on unpaid balance
- EMI: Equal monthly installment, interest calculated on reducing balance

Floating vs Fixed Rates:
- Floating: Linked to base rate, changes quarterly with RBI repo rate
- Fixed: Rate locked at loan origination, doesn't change for loan tenure
- Option to switch: Can convert floating to fixed (or vice versa) after 12 months, no charge

Rate Reset Clause:
- Floating rates reset quarterly (3 months) based on base rate
- Reset date: 1st of every calendar quarter (Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1)
- Customer notification: Email and SMS sent 15 days before reset
- EMI adjusted if balance outstanding, new EMI calculated

ACCOUNT CLOSURE AND FUND TRANSFER
==================================

Closure Eligibility:
- All outstanding loans fully repaid
- All disputes/complaints resolved
- No pending transactions or holds
- No dormancy or legal holds
- Credit card (if attached) fully settled

Closure Procedure:
1. Visit home branch with account holder ID
2. Fill Account Closure Form (available in branch or online)
3. Declare any pending checks or standing instructions to be stopped
4. Request outstanding cheques to be marked "Account Closed"
5. Final statement issued on the spot
6. Outstanding balance transferred to specified account within 5-7 working days

Outstanding Balance Transfer:
- Method: NEFT to customer's other account (in-bank or any bank)
- Charges: Free (waived)
- Account Details needed: Recipient account number, bank name, IFSC code, account holder name
- Timeline: 5-7 working days
- Confirmation: Email and SMS sent when transfer initiates and completes

Closure Charges:
- Standard: Free (no charge)
- Express closure (same-day): Rs. 500 (rarely offered, requires executive approval)
- Multiple closure attempts (>3 in 12 months): May indicate service issues, investigated

Final Statement and Records:
- Final statement issued immediately at branch or within 24 hours if digital request
- Records maintained: 7 years (regulatory requirement)
- Loans/Credit Cards: Account closure doesn't affect linked credit products, must be separately closed
- Safe Deposit Locker: Must be vacated before account closure

Unclaimed Funds:
- If final balance not claimed for 5 years: Transferred to RBI Unclaimed Deposits Fund
- Customer can claim anytime with proof of ownership (death certificate if account holder deceased)
- No interest on claimed funds after transfer to RBI
